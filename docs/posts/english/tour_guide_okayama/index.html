okayama

Okayama Korakuen

Recognized as one of Japan’s “Three Great Gardens,” Okayama Korakuen offers a breathtaking landscape that beautifully portrays Japan’s traditional gardening art. Designed in the 17th century, this garden seamlessly harmonizes various elements, such as vast ponds, meandering streams, elegant teahouses, and seasonally blooming plants. It’s a favorite retreat among locals and tourists alike, especially during the cherry blossom and autumn foliage seasons. The garden’s serene atmosphere is further accentuated with sights of cranes and other birds, adding to its natural charm.

Sasao Bay

Located along the Seto Inland Sea, Sasao Bay is a picturesque coastal spot in Okayama Prefecture. The calm and shimmering waters of the bay, combined with its intricate coastline, make it an ideal location for relaxation and contemplation. Visitors often indulge in beach activities, fishing, or simply gazing at the mesmerizing sunsets. The bay is also known for its delicious seafood, making it a haven for food enthusiasts. Numerous festivals and events take place in the area throughout the year, adding vibrancy to the serene landscape.

Okayama Castle

Often referred to as the “Crow Castle” due to its distinctive black exterior, Okayama Castle stands as an emblem of the region’s rich history and architectural prowess. Constructed in the late 16th century, this castle, although rebuilt after World War II, retains much of its original allure. With its majestic appearance complemented by the serene Asahi River that flows beside it, the castle provides an insightful glimpse into Japan’s feudal past. Inside, visitors can explore several exhibits showcasing samurai artifacts, traditional artworks, and more, offering a deeper understanding of the castle’s historical significance.

Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter

A journey through the Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter feels like stepping back in time. With its beautifully preserved Edo-period (1603-1868) buildings, stone-paved streets, and classic willow-lined canal, this area showcases traditional Japanese beauty at its finest. Once a significant center for rice distribution, many of the old rice granaries and warehouses have been transformed into museums, boutiques, and cafes, each adding to the district’s charm. The reflections of the white-walled storehouses on the still waters of the canal, especially during the golden hours of sunset, make for a picturesque scene, further elevating Kurashiki’s status as one of Japan’s most scenic towns.

Kibi Road

Kibi Road is an ancient path in Okayama Prefecture that has been in existence since ancient times. It’s a must-visit for history enthusiasts. This route was once bustling as a crucial hub for transportation and trade, and it’s believed that many cultures and techniques were passed down here. Walking along this old path allows visitors to sense the lifestyle and landscape of ancient times, offering a different ambiance compared to the modern era. The surrounding area is dotted with historic shrines and temples, allowing visitors to enjoy both history and nature.

Tegarayama

Tegarayama is a nature-rich area with easy access from Okayama city, making it a recommended spot for those wanting a break from urban hustle and bustle. From the summit, one can enjoy a panoramic view of Okayama city, especially the stunning night view. The area attracts many families coming for hiking or picnics, and one can observe the seasonal flora and fauna. Moreover, the Tegarayama Shrine is also worth a visit, drawing many worshippers with their prayers and wishes.

Asahikawa

Asahikawa is a picturesque river flowing through Okayama Prefecture, offering a variety of seasonal beauty. In spring, cherry blossoms bloom in abundance. During summer, a refreshing breeze sweeps through, and in autumn, the fall foliage paints a beautiful landscape. In winter, the serene scenery is blanketed in snow. The river also offers water sports like canoeing and kayaking, making it a go-to destination for those seeking an active day out.

Seto Ohashi Bridge

The Seto Ohashi Bridge is a colossal structure connecting Okayama Prefecture with Kagawa Prefecture and is recognized as a symbol of the region. Spanning approximately 13.1 km, this bridge caters to cars, trains, and pedestrians, acting as a vital link between Shikoku and Honshu. The nighttime illumination of the bridge is particularly mesmerizing, attracting numerous tourists and photographers. Nearby facilities offer insights into the bridge’s construction history and engineering, ensuring an informative experience for visitors of all ages.
